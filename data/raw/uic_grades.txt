Title: UIC Computer Science Grade Trends

Course: CS 141 - Programming I
Observations:
- Introductory programming course.
- Large enrollment every semester.
- Student success often depends on instructor and prior programming experience.
- Frequently discussed as a foundation for later CS courses.

Course: CS 151 - Mathematical Foundations of Computing
Observations:
- Important prerequisite for many upper-level CS courses.
- Students often recommend taking strong instructors such as Bello or McCarty.
- Heavy emphasis on logic and discrete mathematics.

Course: CS 211 - Programming Practicum
Observations:
- Commonly taken alongside CS 251.
- Project-based course with significant coding assignments.
- Requires strong time management.

Course: CS 251 - Data Structures
Observations:
- Frequently described as one of the most important CS courses.
- Covers trees, graphs, hash tables, sorting, and searching.
- Students consider it valuable but time consuming.

Course: CS 261 - Machine Organization
Observations:
- Frequently described as challenging.
- Covers assembly language and computer architecture.
- Students often report significant workload.

Course: CS 301 - Languages and Automata
Observations:
- Covers automata theory and formal languages.
- Students often report positive experiences with Bello and McCarty.
- Important theoretical CS course.

Course: CS 341 - Programming Language Design
Observations:
- Student experiences vary significantly by instructor.
- Historically associated with criticism when taught by certain professors.
- Covers language design concepts and functional programming.

Course: CS 401 - Computer Algorithms
Observations:
- One of the most important upper-level CS courses.
- Difficulty depends heavily on instructor.
- Strong preparation for technical interviews and advanced coursework.
