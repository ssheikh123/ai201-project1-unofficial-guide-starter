Additional Perspectives on the UIC Computer Science Program

Students frequently describe UIC Computer Science as a practical and affordable option for earning a CS degree.

Strengths often mentioned:
- Location in Chicago.
- Access to internships.
- Research opportunities.
- Large course selection.
- Affordable tuition compared to many universities.

Challenges often mentioned:
- Variation in professor quality.
- Heavy workloads in certain courses.
- Competitive student culture in some classes.
- Significant self-learning requirements.

Preparation for Industry

Many students felt that coursework alone was not enough to secure internships or full-time jobs. Students frequently supplemented their education through personal projects, hackathons, research experiences, and technical interview preparation.

Recommended activities included:
- Building portfolio projects.
- Participating in student organizations.
- Attending networking events.
- Contributing to open-source software.
- Practicing coding interview questions.

Students generally agreed that combining coursework with practical experience improved career outcomes significantly.
