Title: UIC Grade Distribution and Course Difficulty Notes

CS 141
Difficulty Level: Introductory
Notes:
- Entry-level programming course.
- Grade outcomes vary significantly by instructor.
- Frequently taken by first-year CS students.

CS 151
Difficulty Level: Moderate
Notes:
- Mathematical foundations course.
- Strong predictor of success in later theoretical CS courses.

CS 211
Difficulty Level: Moderate
Notes:
- Programming-intensive course.
- Students often pair it with CS 251.

CS 251
Difficulty Level: Moderate to High
Notes:
- Core data structures course.
- Considered one of the most valuable courses in the curriculum.
- Significant project workload.

CS 261
Difficulty Level: High
Notes:
- Frequently described by students as one of the harder required CS courses.
- Heavy focus on low-level computing concepts.

CS 301
Difficulty Level: Moderate
Notes:
- Theory-focused course.
- Covers automata and formal languages.
- Generally viewed more positively with certain instructors.

CS 341
Difficulty Level: High
Notes:
- Student experience varies widely depending on instructor.
- Historically receives mixed reviews.

CS 401
Difficulty Level: High
Notes:
- Algorithms course.
- Important preparation for technical interviews.
- Difficulty varies by professor and semester.
