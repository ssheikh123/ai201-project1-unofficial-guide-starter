Title: RateMyProfessor UIC Computer Science Summary

Professor: Gonzalo Bello
Overall Rating: 4.9/5
Would Take Again: 98%
Student Feedback:
- One of the highest rated CS professors at UIC.
- Clear explanations and organized lectures.
- Fair grading and approachable personality.
- Frequently recommended for CS 151 and CS 301.

Professor: Joseph Hummel
Overall Rating: 3.8/5
Would Take Again: 58%
Student Feedback:
- Strong knowledge of data structures and systems.
- Helpful during office hours.
- Students who attend lectures and start projects early often succeed.
- Some students consider his courses difficult and workload heavy.

Professor: Jon Solworth
Overall Rating: 2.1/5
Would Take Again: 24%
Student Feedback:
- Frequently criticized for disorganized lectures.
- Students report outdated materials and confusing course structure.
- Common complaints involve CS 341.
- Some positive reviews praise his resources and teaching assistants.

Professor: Sara Riazi
Student Feedback:
- Mixed reviews from students.
- Common complaints involve communication and lecture clarity.
- Limited reviews for upper-level courses such as CS 480.
- Newer faculty member compared to many UIC professors.

Professor: Danko
Student Feedback:
- Frequently described as approachable and personable.
- Fair grading policies.
- Students report manageable workload.
- Positive reputation among students taking advanced courses.

Professor: McCarty
Student Feedback:
- Often recommended for CS 151.
- Clear lectures and supportive teaching style.
- Frequently praised by students on Reddit discussions.
- Considered one of the stronger introductory CS instructors.

Professor: Mobasheri
Student Feedback:
- Strong reviews from students in CS 401.

- Frequently listed among favorite professors by graduating students.
